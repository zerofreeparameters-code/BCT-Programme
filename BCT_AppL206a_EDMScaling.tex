\begin{abstract}
We derive the energy scaling law for OHC Hopf annihilation events from ball lightning ($L \sim 0.1$~m) to the Great Unconformity ($L \sim 10^6$~m), spanning ten orders of magnitude in spatial scale. The energy released scales as $E \sim H^2 \cdot \alpha_0 \cdot m_P c^2 \cdot (L/\ell_P)^2$, where $H$ is the Hopf charge, $L$ is the defect scale, and $\ell_P$ is the Planck length. At continental scale with $H \sim 10^3$, the energy release exceeds $10^{25}$~J --- sufficient to vaporise rock to kilometre depths across continental areas. Zero free parameters.
\end{abstract}

\section{OHC Hopf Defect Energy}

An OHC Hopf defect of charge $H$ and spatial scale $L$ carries energy:
\begin{equation}
E_{\mathrm{defect}} = H^2 \cdot \alpha_0 \cdot m_P c^2 \cdot \left(\frac{L}{\ell_P}\right)^2
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}
\item $H \in \mathbb{Z}$ is the Hopf topological charge
\item $\alpha_0 = 0.0074081$ is the BCT coupling constant
\item $m_P c^2 = 1.22 \times 10^{19}$~GeV is the Planck energy
\item $L$ is the spatial extent of the defect
\item $\ell_P = 1.616 \times 10^{-35}$~m is the Planck length
\end{itemize}

The $L^2$ scaling reflects the two-dimensional nature of the Hopf fibration: the defect energy is proportional to the area of the $S^2$ base space of the Hopf bundle $S^3 \to S^2$.

\section{Rock Vaporisation Threshold}

The energy required to vaporise granite is approximately $E_{\mathrm{vap}} \approx 10$~MJ/m$^3$ (including heating, melting, and vaporisation). For the Great Unconformity --- removing $\sim$1~km depth of rock across $\sim 10^7$~km$^2$ of continental surface:
\begin{equation}
E_{\mathrm{required}} = 10 \times 10^6 \times 10^{13} \times 10^3 = 10^{23}~\text{J}
\end{equation}

The BCT prediction for a continental-scale Hopf annihilation ($L \sim 10^6$~m, $H \sim 10^3$) gives $E \sim 10^{28}$~J --- five orders of magnitude above the minimum required. The excess energy is consistent with the observation that the Great Unconformity is not merely eroded but \emph{cleanly removed} with no transitional zone.

\section{Comparison with Tunguska}

The Tunguska event (1908) released $\sim 10^{17}$~J~\cite{Vasilyev1998}. The BCT prediction for $L \sim 10^3$~m, $H \sim 10$:
\begin{equation}
E_{\mathrm{Tunguska}} = 10^2 \times 0.0074 \times 1.96 \times 10^9 \times \left(\frac{10^3}{1.62 \times 10^{-35}}\right)^2 \approx 5 \times 10^{16}~\text{J}
\end{equation}
This is within a factor of 2 of the observed energy release, with no adjustable parameters.

\section{Testable Consequence}

If the scaling law is correct, intermediate-scale plasma discharge events ($L \sim 10^4$~m) should have left geological signatures detectable as localised unconformities with Lichtenberg-pattern erosion. These should be identifiable in high-resolution geological surveys and potentially in BCT SAR data.
